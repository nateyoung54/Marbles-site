History & Culture

Over 130 years of American craftsmanship. All steel. All American. Still made in Gladstone, Michigan.

Where It All Began

In 1887, a young inventor named Webster L. Marble arrived in Gladstone, Michigan, drawn by the timber industry and the vast wilderness of the Upper Peninsula. But Webster wasn't just there to cut trees—he was there to solve problems.

Five years later, in 1892, Marble founded his company with a simple mission: build the tools that outdoorsmen actually need, built to last a lifetime. His first breakthrough was the Universal Rifle Sight—a revolutionary design that could be adjusted to fit virtually any rifle. It was an instant success.

By 1912, Marble's advertising reached over 20 million readers worldwide. The company had become synonymous with quality outdoor equipment, from their famous safety axes to pocket compasses carried by Theodore Roosevelt himself on his expeditions.

A Legacy of Innovation

What set Marble apart from the beginning wasn't just quality—it was innovation. Webster Marble held dozens of patents for everything from game-getting devices to safety mechanisms. He didn't just make products; he invented entire categories.

The Marble's Game Getter, a combination rifle and shotgun, became legendary among trappers and surveyors. The Marble's safety axe, with its folding blade guard, was the first of its kind. And the Universal Rifle Sight set the standard that iron sights are still measured against today.

Through two World Wars, the Great Depression, and countless economic cycles, Marble Arms kept making products the same way: all steel, precision machined, built to outlast the shooter.

Still Here. Still Building.

Today, Marble Arms remains in Gladstone, Michigan—the same town where Webster Marble started it all over 130 years ago. The machines are newer, but the commitment is the same: all steel construction, precision CNC machining, and a refusal to cut corners.

We're proud to be the OEM sight supplier for many of America's most respected firearms manufacturers. When gun makers need sights they can trust, they call Marble Arms. That trust has been earned over generations, one precision sight at a time.

From the original Universal Rifle Sight to today's fiber optic technology, we've never stopped improving. But we've also never forgotten what made us successful in the first place: build it right, build it from steel, build it in America.

Small Town Values, World-Class Quality

Gladstone, Michigan sits on the shores of Little Bay de Noc in the Upper Peninsula. It's a place where people know their neighbors, where word-of-mouth reputation matters, and where cutting corners isn't just bad business—it's personal.

That small-town accountability is built into everything we do. When you buy a Marble Arms sight, you're not just buying from a company—you're buying from the people who made it, who live down the street from each other, and who take personal pride in their work.

Many of our team members are hunters and shooters themselves. They use the products they build, and they build them to the standard they'd demand for their own firearms.
