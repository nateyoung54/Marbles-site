Our History

The Long Game

From a one-man operation in 1892 to a full-scale CNC facility, the commitment hasn't changed.

1892

Founded in Gladstone, Michigan

Webster L. Marble manufactures the Universal Rifle Sight and incorporates as Gladstone Manufacturing Company, setting a new benchmark for iron sight precision from day one.

1898

The Patent That Built a Brand

Marble patents the Safety Pocket Axe and reincorporates as Marble Safety Axe Company. A product built to last becomes the foundation of a brand built to last even longer.

1909

Finest Factory in the World

A new 24,000 square foot factory is completed in Gladstone and immediately lauded as the "finest of its kind in the world." Marble Arms scales to meet national demand without leaving home.

1913

Roosevelt's Choice

President Theodore Roosevelt carries Marble's compasses into the uncharted Brazilian wilderness on his scientific expedition. When the world's most famous adventurer trusts your gear with his life, the reputation speaks for itself.

1927

Across the Atlantic with Lindbergh

Charles Lindbergh packs Marble's knife, compass, and matchbox into the Spirit of St. Louis for the first solo transatlantic flight. Marble Arms earns its place as gear trusted at the edge of human achievement.

1998

A Classic Returns

The Peep Tang Sight is reintroduced with windage adjustment, bringing a beloved design back for a new generation of hunters and competitive shooters who know that the best ideas don't expire.

2000

Fiber Optic Innovation

Marble Arms adds fiber optic front, rear, and shotgun sights to the product line, pairing over a century of iron sight expertise with modern aiming technology for today's shooters.

2007

Improved Peep Tang Sight

The Improved Peep Tang Sight launches with interchangeable uprights, giving shooters a versatile, customizable precision sight, the latest chapter in a product line with more than a century of refinement behind it.
